Understanding Color Coordination

Color coordination is the key to creating visually appealing outfits. By understanding color theory and how different colors work together, you can elevate your style and make a statement with your wardrobe.

1. Complementary Colors

Pair colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel, like blue and orange or red and green. This creates a bold and striking contrast.

2. Analogous Colors

Combine colors that are next to each other on the color wheel, like blue, teal, and green. This creates a harmonious and cohesive look.

3. Monochromatic Outfits

Stick to different shades of the same color for a sleek and sophisticated outfit. For example, pair light pink with dark pink.

4. Neutral Colors

Use neutral colors like black, white, beige, and gray as a base. They pair well with almost any color and add balance to your outfit.

5. Triadic Colors

Choose three colors that are evenly spaced on the color wheel, like yellow, blue, and red. This creates a vibrant and dynamic look.

6. Accent Colors

Add a pop of color to a neutral outfit with a bright accent piece, like a red handbag or yellow shoes.
